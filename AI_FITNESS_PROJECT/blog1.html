SLEEP HOURS

Getting an adequate amount of sleep is crucial for overall health and well-being. Most adults require between 7 to 9 hours of quality sleep each night to function optimally. Sleep plays a vital role in various bodily functions, including cognitive function, emotional well-being, immune system regulation, and metabolism. Lack of sleep or poor sleep quality can lead to a range of health issues, including fatigue, impaired concentration, mood disturbances, weakened immune system, and increased risk of chronic diseases such as obesity, diabetes, and heart disease. To ensure you get enough sleep, establish a consistent sleep schedule, create a relaxing bedtime routine, and maintain a comfortable sleep environment free from distractions.
